2016

Square One.

Square One marked the highly anticipated debut of YGE's next girl group, eight years following 2NE1's debut. The single included "Whistle" and "Boombayah," enjoyed critical acclaim, dominating the Gaon Digital Chart in Korea and the US World Digital Songs chart within their first weeks of release.

Distinguishing themselves from other groups, BLACKPINK debuted with two music videos, each amassing over 10 million views in just five days.

This sensational debut propelled BLACKPINK into the limelight, earning them the title of "monster rookies" from both industry experts and fans alike.

Square Two.

BLACKPINK's second comeback came just two months after the debut. Square two marked a change from their first single, having a ballad such as Stay, and adding an acoustic version of their debut song, Whistle.

Just like Square one, Square Two dominated the charts world-wide, spending 22 weeks on the BillBoard World Album chart. To date, it sits as the 8th most streamed album by a female K-pop artist.

STAY became an anthem for the group, being a way to call their fans to keep supporting them.

2017

As If It's Your Last.

As If It's Your Last marked the only BLACPINK's release of 2017. It came seven months after SQUARE TWO, and was a massive success. The song was regarded as a gift for BLINKS as the group prepared for their next album.

The video for the single gathered 11 million views in less than seventeen hours, breaking the previous record of 17 million views in 21 hours. It topped all the charts worldwide, becoming an instant classic of Kpop.

2018

Square Up.

Square UP was the first BLACKPINK'S mini album, and their highly anticipated comeback after a year-long hiatus. Its concept was more mature, its title showing BLACKPINK's readiness to stand up to any challenge.

This mini album catapulted BLACKPINK to the upper echelons of KPOP and music charts. Its main track, DDU-DU DDU-DU, peaked in 44 different countries, was featured in the videogame Just Dance 2019, and awarded them Gold certification in the US, being the first female Kpop group to earn it.

Solo.

SOLO was the first solo single from BLACKPINK. Jennie debuted the song during their "In Your Area" Tour in Seoul.

The song topped the Itunes chart in 40 different countries, making it the first kpop female solo artist to do so.

SOLO quickly became an anthem for female empowerment, talking about leaving fragility after a break up, embracing oneself and finding one's own light.

The MV for the song became only the second song for a solo kpop artist to reach 1 billion views.

2019

Kill This Love.

After another year-long hiatus, YGE announced BLACKPINK's next mini-album, describing its lead single to be "more intense" than previous songs.

Kill This Love's lyrics talked about fighting the weakness within oneself. The choreography was a lot more dynamic than previous comebacks, featuring a whole group of dancers.

During this era, BLACKPINK headlined in COACHELLA, being the most talked about act during the whole festival. This album is regarded as one of their best, breaking almost every record they had smashed previously.

2020

The Album.

THE ALBUM was BLACKPINK's first studio album, featuring 8 songs, and two collaborations with American artists, Selena Gomez and Cardi B.

Its promotion was scheduled as a three step part, starting with the release of their pre-single, How You Like that; later releasing their Collab with Selena Gomez, "Ice Cream". Their title song, Lovesick Girls, was later announced.

This album was regarded as more mature, showing a lot more emotions than just love.

During their comeback, all 8 songs debuted in the Spotify Top 25, earning them several nominations to award shows.

2021

-R-.

Rosé's solo debut was announced to happen during their online concert "The Show" during January, 2021; yet the single didn't debut until March.

In contrast to BLACKPINK's vibe, Rosé's album had a more retro/cinematic mood, lyrics being completely in English. Two MVs were released for the single, In The Ground being its title track.

In The Ground talks about going back to Rosé's roots, knowing that even in fame, she's still the same girl that once sang at the church. Gone tells the story of a heartbroken girl, trying to overcome the sadness of abandonment.

Rosé decided to title it R, to showcase the start of her solo career.

Lalisa.

Lisa's solo came month after Rosé's, fulfilling YG's promises of solos.

Her tracks are hip-hop infused dance tracks. Lalisa, the title track, talks about Lisa's own strenght and confidence she found at being able to chose her own name. Money, in contrast, talks about enjoying the fruits of her own work.

Money went viral in different social networks, taking it to the top of music charts worldwide.

2022

Born Pink.

BORN PINK was BLACKPINK's second studio album, including 8 tracks in several different genres.

The era started with the release of the pre-single, Pink Venom; and the collaboration with the videogame PUGB MOBile, with the song Ready to Love, which involved an animated MV and an in-game concert.

With this album, BLACKPINK annouced their future namesake world tour.

2023

Me.

Jisoo's single closed the first round of solos for the four members.

Its title track, Flower, talks about an ending relationship, where the girl takes it as a signal to enjoy her new found freedom. The b-track, All Eyes on Me, talks about a girl's desire to be praised, wanting to be the center of everyone's attention, just like idols.

Me was a long-awaited solo, which Jisoo at some point said was delayed because she didn't feel ready to record it.

Born Pink Tour.

The BORN PINK tour occupied the last two years of BLACKPINK's career.

With 42 shows in 4 different continents, it's reported to be one of the highest-grossing female artist shows in history.

During this tour, Jennie revealed a special single, called You and Me, which was released after the tour ended, June 2023.
