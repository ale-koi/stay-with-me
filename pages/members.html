Jisoo

Lead Vocalist, Visual.

January, 3rd.

Kim Ji-soo, born in Gunpo, Gyeonggi, South Korea, embarked on her journey with YG Entertainment in 2011 after successful auditions as a trainee. In 2015, she made a cameo appearance in the KBS2 drama "The Producers" alongside label-mates 2NE1's Sandara Park and Winner's Kang Seung-yoon, while also featuring in various advertisements including Samsonite, Smart Uniform, LG Electronics, and Nikon.

Joining YG Entertainment as a trainee at 16 in August 2011, Jisoo dedicated five years to honing her singing talents. Renowned for her beauty even before joining YG, she gained popularity in school for her stunning looks and talents, initially attending the School of Performing Arts High School before relocating to Seoul, South Korea.

In 2012, Jisoo was introduced through YG Entertainment's "Who's That Girl?" teaser, followed by photo releases in January the following year.

On August 18, 2020, YG Entertainment announced Jisoo's acting debut, where she will lead the upcoming JTBC drama "Snowdrop," expected to air in 2021. Additionally, on December 30, 2020, it was revealed that Jisoo would make her solo debut after the completion of filming for "Snowdrop."

In Gallup Korea's annual music poll for 2018, Jisoo ranked as the tenth most popular idol in South Korea, while in April 2019, she was listed as the 10th most followed K-pop idol on Instagram, boasting 12.8 million followers. Globally recognized for her nurturing personality, Jisoo is often referred to as the 'mom of the group' and 'unofficial leader' due to her exemplary leadership skills and caring nature.

Jennie

Lead singer, rapper.

January, 16th.

Jennie Kim, the sole child of her parents, hails from a distinguished background; her mother serves as a Director and Shareholder at CJ & M, while her father is a hospital owner. Spending her formative years in Auckland, New Zealand, Jennie attended ACG Parnell College, where she honed her language skills, mastering English alongside basic Japanese and her native Korean.

Relocating to Seoul, South Korea in 2010, Jennie embarked on her journey with YG Entertainment after a successful audition, dedicating six years to rigorous training as a YG trainee. During this period, she showcased her talents by featuring in collaborations with labelmates such as Lee Hi and G-Dragon.

In 2016, Jennie emerged as the main rapper and lead vocalist of the groundbreaking girl group, BLACKPINK, which debuted to widespread acclaim. Her solo career skyrocketed in mid-October 2018, with the announcement of her official solo debut and the subsequent release of her chart-topping single, "Solo." The song shattered records, becoming the most-viewed music video by a female K-pop solo artist within 24 hours and surpassing 300 million YouTube views within six months.

Continuing to break barriers, Jennie made headlines in 2022 with news of her Hollywood debut in the television series "The Idol," created by Abel "The Weeknd" Tesfaye, Reza Fahim, and Sam Levinson for HBO, solidifying her status as the first Korean idol to feature in an American drama series.

Rosé

Main vocalist, lead dancer.

February, 11th

Rosé's Korean name is Park Chaeyoung and her English name is Roseanne Park. She was born in Auckland, New Zealand and raised in Melbourne, Australia. As a child, Rosé always had a passion for singing and was a part of her church's choir.

In 2012, Rosé's father found out that YG Entertainment was holding their auditions in Sydney, Australia. He convinced her to take part in it. Rosé ranked first in the auditions, beating 700 others, and became a trainee on the same day. After training for four years, YG finally put her as the main vocalist and lead dancer of BLACKPINK. She was the last member to be revealed in the band.Less than 6 months after joining YG, Rosé featured on G-Dragon's One of a Kind EP, on the track "Without You".

On January 25, 2021, it was reported that Rosé will be performing a song from her solo album for the first time at BLACKPINK's upcoming concert BLACKPINK: The Show. At the concert, Rosé performed the song heard in the coming soon teaser. After her live performance, the title of the song was revealed to be "Gone". The track consists of 100% English lyrics and is about a woman's broken heart.

Rosé's voice has received acknowledgment in the K-Pop industry for its distinct vocal timbre. Following her debut as a member of Blackpink, Rosé's vocal color was largely met with praise. Filipina critic Almira Blancada, writing for ABS-CBN, stated that "it's hard to find an idol [singer] who stands out from the rest" due to their "common singing style", but "you would know it's Rosé singing in a heartbeat".

In August 2021, Rosé was the third most-followed K-Pop idol on Instagram with over 43 million followers. Rosé has appeared on the Korean Business Research Institute's female celebrity brand reputation list since 2018, a chart that tracks the Korean celebrities with the most online searches and engagements. She has also reached the top 20.

Rosé also has a successful independent career, amassing a large fan following for herself as a soloist. She is best known as a soloist for her unique vocal timbre, largely receiving praise for its distinctness following her debut.

Lisa

Main dancer, lead rapper, maknae.

March, 27th

Lisa was born Pranpriya Manoban and she later legally changed her first name to Lalisa. Her ethnicity and nationality is Thai. Lisa's biological parents' names have not been released to the public. Swiss renowned chef Marco Bruschweiler is her stepfather, who is currently active in Thailand.

As a child, Lisa was a model and dancer. She made a successful audition to the YG Entertainment Competition in 2010, when she was only 13 years old. She ranked first place in said competition, and was offered by Yang Hyun-suk to become a YG Entertainment trainee, to which Lisa agreed.

In 2011, she moved to South Korea to begin her formal training in performing for her K-Pop Idol career, which lasted five years. During this time, she was a member of a dance group named We Zaa Cool along with fellow Thai K-Pop Idol trainees, Bambam and Ten.

In 2016, she took on her stage name of Lisa and became the main dancer, lead rapper, a supporting vocalist and the maknae of K-pop girl group Blackpink, which debuted on August 8, 2016. Lisa is also the first non-Korean YG Entertainment artist.

On April 18, 2021, Harper’s Bazaar Thailand Instagram account stated that Lisa's solo is set to be released in June. At this time, no official statement has been said by YG. On May 19, 2021, DJ Snake posted a teaser on his Instagram story that he will be collaborating with Lisa. Lisa revealed to Vogue Korea that her solo album will be released in the near future.

On August 25, 2021, two teasers were released on BLACKPINK’s social media accounts. Lisa’s debut album Lalisa is set to be released on September 10, 2021.

In 2018, Lisa won The Standard "Person of the Year" award in Thailand
